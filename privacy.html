Ledgr-Journal

Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 28, 2026

Your financial data belongs to you. Ledgr-Journal does not collect, sell, or share your personal information with third parties.

1. Overview

Ledgr-Journal ("the App", "we", "our") is a personal finance journaling iOS app developed by Noah Yao. This Privacy Policy explains how the App handles your data when you use it.

2. Data We Access

Ledgr-Journal requests access to the following Google account data:

  • Google Sheets — to read from and write journal entries, transactions, and financial records to spreadsheets that you own.
  • Google account email address and profile — solely to identify your account and display your name within the App.

We only access Google Sheets files that you explicitly connect or create through the App. We do not access any other files in your Google Drive or Google account.

3. How We Use Your Data

Data accessed through your Google account is used exclusively to:

  • Read and display your existing financial journal entries within the App.
  • Write new entries, transactions, and updates that you create in the App to your connected Google Sheet.
  • Authenticate you securely using Google Sign-In.

We do not use your data for advertising, analytics, profiling, or any purpose beyond the core functionality described above.

4. Data Storage & Security

Ledgr-Journal does not operate its own servers. Your financial data lives in your own Google Sheets, under your own Google account. The App communicates directly with Google APIs using OAuth 2.0.

We do not store, cache, or transmit your spreadsheet data to any third-party server. All data processing happens on your device or within your own Google account.

5. Google API Scopes

Ledgr-Journal uses the following Google API scopes:

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/spreadsheets — to read and write your financial journal data.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.email — to retrieve your email for account identification.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.profile — to retrieve your display name.

Our use of Google API data is in compliance with the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

6. Third-Party Services

The App uses Google Sign-In and the Google Sheets API, both provided by Google LLC. Your use of these services is subject to Google's Privacy Policy.

No other third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs are included in the App.

7. Children's Privacy

Ledgr-Journal is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated revision date. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
